# Code of Conduct

## Purpose

This project aims to support open, rigorous, and collaborative scientific research. We value constructive discussion, reproducible work, and respectful collaboration among contributors with diverse expertise and viewpoints.

## Expected Behavior

Contributors are expected to:

- Engage respectfully and professionally in all project spaces
- Focus discussions on scientific, technical, and methodological topics
- Provide constructive feedback and respond to criticism in good faith
- Credit prior work appropriately and cite relevant sources
- Follow accepted standards of academic and research integrity

## Research & Code Integrity

All contributions must:

- Be original or properly attributed
- Comply with the projectâ€™s license and applicable open-source licenses
- Avoid inclusion of proprietary, confidential, or copyrighted material without explicit permission
- Not introduce code, data, or dependencies that could create legal, licensing, or ethical concerns

Contributors are responsible for ensuring they have the right to submit any material they contribute.

## Unacceptable Behavior

Unacceptable behavior includes, but is not limited to:

- Harassment, personal attacks, or unprofessional conduct
- Misrepresentation of data, results, or authorship
- Knowingly submitting plagiarized or improperly licensed material
- Disruptive behavior that undermines productive scientific discussion

## Scope & Enforcement

This Code of Conduct applies to all project-related spaces, including repositories, issues, pull requests, and discussions.

Project maintainers may remove or reject contributions or restrict participation if this Code of Conduct is violated. Enforcement actions will be proportional and focused on maintaining the integrity and usability of the project.
